Webhooks are how external systems push events **into** Oblea. Unlike the platform endpoints under `/integrations`, `/inbox`, and `/quotations` — which the frontend calls on behalf of a signed-in user — webhooks are machine-to-machine ingress: another system in the pipeline delivers an event, and Oblea reacts.

## The one webhook today

There is a single inbound webhook. It is called by `inbound.new` whenever an email arrives at one of a company's forwarding addresses.

<Warning>
  Webhooks are **unauthenticated** today. They perform no token check and simply trust their caller — they are reachable only because `inbound.new` is configured with the service URL. See [Authentication](/authentication) for the full picture and the intended hardening path.
</Warning>

## What `receive_message` triggers

When an email lands, the webhook runs a short pipeline:

1. **Match** the recipient address against `company_emails` to resolve the owning company.
2. **Classify** the message with OpenAI — is this a quote request?
3. **Store** a `messages` row with the raw payload and the classifier's verdict.
4. **Maybe create** a `quotation` (status `PROCESSING`) and **hand off** to `oblea-agent`, which fills the draft in the background.

The full end-to-end journey — from the customer's email all the way to a `READY TO REVIEW` quotation a human approves — is traced in [The quotation lifecycle](/concepts/quotation-lifecycle). This webhook is steps 1 through 7 of it.

## The other internal endpoint

There is a second machine-to-machine endpoint in the pipeline, but it is not a webhook in this group: [`POST /quotations/{id}`](/quotations/store-agent-draft), where `oblea-agent` hands the filled draft back to the backend. It is documented under **Quotations**. Like `receive_message`, it is unauthenticated and trusts its caller.
